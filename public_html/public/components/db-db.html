Dorm Buddy - Overview

Background

In the beginning of November 2017, 2 months into our Mobile Application Development (CS477) course, we got started on the semester's main project, developing an Android application. I decided to partner up with Vincent Liu after hearing his idea of having a "laundry reservation app," where students can reserve laundry machines ahead of time so that they save a trip to the laundromat when all machines are full. I really liked this idea, but at the same time, wanted to expand it into much more. After a lot of discussion and high level talks, we settled on having not just a laundry reservation system, but a general application that would help dorm students with their college experience. We dubbed the app "Dorm Buddy," and decided that having "buddies" would be a recurring theme in the app.

The Idea

Dorm Buddy's ERP Diagram
The first thing we did was write up an architecture document. Our architecture document included the app's features, activities, and relations. The document also discussed the necessary database tables, servers/services, and a storyboard from a user's point of view. The gists of the architecture document are as follows:
  • The app will have 5 different major features, called buddies. Those are:
    • Laundry Buddy - a laundry machine reservation system
    • Map Buddy - a local navigation system
    • Study Buddy - a study room reservation system
    • Event Buddy - an event room reservation and event broadcasting system
    • Profile Buddy - a user information holder
  • There will be two personas: a student and an administrator. Administrators, such as Resident Advisors, would have more application privileges than an average dorm tenant.
  • Firebase will be used as our database service. Data stored in Firebase would include dorm buildings, their maps, their rooms, laundry machine, and most importantly, the reservations made.
  • Some static database data would also be copied to the user's phone for convenience. Such data would include the user's profile info, the laundry machines available for their specific building, and dorm floor maps/university maps.
  • Other data stored on the user's phone would be their laundry templates, their upcoming reservations,
