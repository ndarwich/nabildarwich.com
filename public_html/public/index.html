Featured Project - Nabil's Books

One of my favorite courses in GMU's Computer Science Master's program is CS 672 (Computer Systems Performance Evaluation). By taking this course, I have learned very important laws that govern how computers are expected to behave. Computer systems do not behave arbitrarily; if a computer is slow, there is a physical reason why it is so, and that reason very likely is that a computer resource (be it hardware components or software regions) is being heavily utilized. Now, on a small scale, such as having a slow personal laptop, an individual can simply replace the laptop with a newer one and expect it to be faster. That individual is myself after I grew tired of a very slow 2-core 1 TB HDD laptop I abused during my undergrad, purchasing a new laptop in December 2017 that I use to this day. I invested more than $800 on this laptop, and it has a 512 GB SSD and a 2.6 GHz 4-core i7-6700HQ processor. It's fast. Very fast. Back to the lesson; on a large scale, this idea works as well, but a lot more than $800 is going to be involved. And that is why I founded Nabil's Books.
