How do I do it?

Prior to starting my undergrad, I had no experience writing code. In fact, the only reason why I decided to study Computer Science at the time was for the simple fact that I loved messing around with computers, and I was my family's free tech support specialist. So, I chose Computer Science as my major and took at least 3 courses every semester that are relevant to the field and are designated to build up my knowledge. After my first successful year, I wanted to give back, and help students just like I was helped when I was new to the field. So, I applied to become a teaching assistant, and made it in the department! My first semester as a teaching assistant was invaluable, as I had over 1,500 contributions in the shared online forum, and I made unforgettable friendships with the students I helped. That spirit of selflessness was very rewarding, as it helped me refresb my knowledge on topics I previously studied, and helped students clear up hazy new topics, so it was a win-win. I continued on assisting the department, and my students grew their knowledge with me. Now, my students are working for some of the highest paying tech firms, and I'm over here, continuing my learning and improving my knowledge, as I'm just fascinated by the possibilities this field opens up.
