Featured Project - Nabil's Books

One of my favorite GMU Computer Science graduate courses is CS 672 (Computer Systems Performance Evaluation). By taking this course, I have learned very important laws that govern how computers are expected to behave. Computer systems do not behave arbitrarily; if a computer is slow, there is a physical reason why it is so, whether it's hardware or thread contention. On a small scale, the solution is rather trivial: just get a new computer with better specs. On a large scale, an executive decision that may incur a lot of costs has to be made. The class explores multiple case studies that involve this decision making, and for my course project, I created a website for a fictional online bookstore.
Nabil's Books
Nabil's Books is a world-renowned bookstore that serves knowledge to millions of people. Knowledge comes in many file formats, and Nabil's books sells Text Books (.txt), Image Books (.png), Audio Books (.mp3), Video Books (.mp4), and Miscellaneous Books (.zip/.ppt/.pdf). In order to boost customer satisfaction, Nabil wants to ensure that consumers are getting the service they expect in a timely manner. And so, an executive decision has to be made regarding whether to purchase more CPUs or more Disks for the server that hosts Nabil's Books.
